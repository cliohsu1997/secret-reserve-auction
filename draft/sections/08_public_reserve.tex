\section{Case of Public Reserve Price}
\label{sec:public-reserve}

If the equilibrium is separating, the low-cost seller sets a reserve price given
by
\begin{equation}
  r_L - c_L
  = (1 - \delta)
    \frac{
      1 - F\!\left(\dfrac{r_L - \delta c_L}{1 - \delta}\right)
    }{
      f\!\left(\dfrac{r_L - \delta c_L}{1 - \delta}\right)
    }
  - \frac{1}{F(r_L)^{N-1}}
    \int_{r_L}^{(r_L - \delta c_L)/(1 - \delta)}
      (v - c_L)\, dF(v)^{N-1}.
  \label{eq:FOC-low-public}
\end{equation}

whereas the high-cost seller sets reserve price weakly above $r_H$, solution to
\begin{equation}
  r_H - c_H
  = (1 - \delta)
    \frac{
      1 - F\!\left(\dfrac{r_H - \delta c_H}{1 - \delta}\right)
    }{
      f\!\left(\dfrac{r_H - \delta c_H}{1 - \delta}\right)
    }
  - \frac{1}{F(r_H)^{N-1}}
    \int_{r_H}^{(r_H - \delta c_H)/(1 - \delta)}
      (v - c_H)\, dF(v)^{N-1},
  \label{eq:FOC-high-public}
\end{equation}
that is enough to deter low-type mimicking.
