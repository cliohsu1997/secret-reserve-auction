\section{Model Outline}
\label{sec:model}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item One seller, $N$ bidders.
  \item Seller's cost is either $c_L$ or $c_H$ with $\Pr(c_L) = \psi$.
  \item Buyer's value is i.i.d.\ distributed according to some $F$ supported on
    $[c_L, 1]$, with density $f$.
  \item \textbf{First stage:} an English auction with initially hidden reserve
    price, which is revealed conditional on being met.
  \item \textbf{Second stage:} if the English auction fails, one bidder is
    randomly drawn to make a TIOLI (take-it-or-leave-it) offer to the seller.
    An individual bidder is drawn with probability $\delta \le 1/N$.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Notation.}
$\underline{r}$ and $\bar{r}$: endogenous lower and upper support of the
low-cost seller's mixed strategy (chosen in equilibrium, not drawn);
$r_H$: high-cost seller's reserve;
$\rho \in \Delta[\underline{r}, \bar{r}]$: cdf of the reserve $r$ actually
played on the path;
$\beta$: symmetric stage-1 bidding strategy; $\tilde{\psi}$: belief that the
seller is low-cost.
